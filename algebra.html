Algebra Course




About the Course

This is an Algebra course for beginners. It covers the fundamental concepts of algebra and provides practice exercises to reinforce understanding.

By the end of the course, students should be comfortable working with algebraic expressions, solving equations and inequalities, and graphing linear functions.

Prerequisites: Basic Math knowledge

Course Information

Number of Units: 3

Number of Skills: 30

Time to Complete: 10 weeks

Difficulty Level: Beginner

Unit 1 - Algebraic Expressions

Simplifying expressions Evaluating expressions Translating words to expressions Combining like terms Distributive property Factoring expressions Solving equations with one variable Solving problems with two variables Writing equations to solve word problems Working with exponents and radicals

Unit 2 - Equations and Inequalities

Solving linear equations with one variable Solving systems of linear equations Solving equations with fractions and decimals Solving quadratic equations by factoring Understanding and graphing inequalities Solving systems of linear inequalities Working with absolute value equations and inequalities Solving rational equations Solving radical equations and inequalities Applying equations and inequalities in real-life situations Working with word problems Reviewing for the test

Unit 3 - Graphing Linear Functions

Graphing linear equations Finding slope and y-intercept Using the slope-intercept form Finding equations of lines Using functions to model real-life problems Solving linear systems by graphing Understanding and graphing inequalities Solving systems of inequalities by graphing
